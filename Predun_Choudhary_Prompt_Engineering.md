\## Part 3: Strategic Prompting Techniques



\### 3.1 Chain-of-Thought Prompting

Problem Chosen: Option C

The Problem:

Problem: You have a meeting at 2 PM that requires 1 hour of prep time,

a 30-minute commute, and you need to eat lunch (30 mins) beforehand.

You also have a 45-minute call scheduled at 11 AM. What time should you

start preparing to make everything work?



Without Chain-of-Thought

Prompt Used:

\[INSTRUCTION: You have a meeting at 2 PM that requires 1 hour of prep time, a 30-minute commute, and you need to eat lunch (30 mins) beforehand. You also have a 45-minute call scheduled at 11 AM. What time should you start preparing to make everything work? Give me the final time only with no extra explanation.

ChatGPT's Output:

12:00 PM



With Chain-of-Thought

Prompt Used: You have a meeting at 2 PM that requires 1 hour of prep time, a 30-minute commute, and you need to eat lunch (30 mins) beforehand. You also have a 45-minute call scheduled at 11 AM. What time should you start preparing to make everything work? Let's solve this step by step.

If a train travels 120 km in 2 hours then stops for 30 minutes

then travels another 90 km in 1.5 hours what is its average speed

for the entire journey?



ChatGPT's Output:

Let's work backward from the 2:00 PM meeting:



\* Meeting starts: \*\*2:00 PM\*\*

\* Commute: \*\*30 minutes\*\* → leave by \*\*1:30 PM\*\*

\* Lunch: \*\*30 minutes\*\* before commute → lunch from \*\*1:00 PM to 1:30 PM\*\*

\* Preparation: \*\*1 hour\*\* before lunch → prep from \*\*12:00 PM to 1:00 PM\*\*



You also have a call from \*\*11:00 AM to 11:45 AM\*\*, which ends before the prep period begins.



\*\*Start preparing at 12:00 PM.\*\*



Comparison and Analysis



* The standard prompt forced the LLM to guess the final time instantly, which can cause autoregressive models to miscalculate time subtraction or completely ignore the scheduling constraint of the 11:00 AM call. 
* In contrast, the Chain-of-Thought prompt forced the model to calculate backward chronologically, validating each milestone against the schedule before outputting the final time. CoT helps with complex reasoning because it breaks down a multi-variable math problem into linear, intermediate steps, keeping the model's focus aligned. However, 
* A major limitation observed is that if the model makes a mistake early on—such as subtracting 30 minutes from 1:30 PM and getting 1:15 PM—the rest of the logical sequence will still execute perfectly but result in an entirely incorrect final answer.

\#### Analysis

Hallucinations pose a critical risk to business analytics workflows because LLMs are optimized for linguistic plausibility rather than empirical truth, meaning they will confidently output mathematically or factually flawed data with an authoritative tone. To systematically reduce hallucinations in business intelligence pipelines, developers must implement strict "escape clauses" in systemic prompts that explicitly permit the model to output an uncertainty token or execute an error-handling path when confidence thresholds are not met.

\### 4.3 Limitations \& Responsible Use



Three Limitations to cover:



Reflecting on the practical exercises conducted throughout this portfolio, three fundamental limitations of working with Large Language Models (LLMs) stand out:



Limitation 1: Hallucination — making up information confidently

Limitation 2: Bias — reflecting stereotypes from training data

Limitation 3: reasoning errors, context limits, inconsistency etc



Three Recommendations to cover:



Recommendation 1: Always verify important AI outputs from trusted sources.

Recommendation 2: Strict Operational Boundaries: LLMs are entirely unsuitable for autonomous, unmonitored decision-making roles, such as executing live database writes or processing sensitive, unencrypted Personally Identifiable Information (PII) without strict localized privacy frameworks.

Recommendation 3: Disclose AI use ethically in academic and professional work.
